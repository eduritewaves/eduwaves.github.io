Monetary Policy
The policy laid down by the central bank in order to regulate and manage the money supply and interest rates is termed as monetary policy. Some primary objectives of this policy are regulating liquidity, managing inflation and maintaining price stability.

HISTORY:
Under the Reserve Bank of India, Act,1934 (RBI Act,1934) (as amended in 2016), RBI is entrusted with the responsibility of conducting monetary policy in India with the primary objective of maintaining price stability while keeping in mind the objective of growth. Further history regarding the policy will be discussed in upcoming articles.

OBJECTIVES:
> Maintain inflation rate
> Reduce unemployment
> Regulation of exchange rates

INSTRUMENTS USED IN MONETARY POLICY:
• Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR)
• Statutory Liquidity Ratio (SLR)
• Bank Rate
• Repo Rate (RR)
• Reverse Repo Rate (RRR)
• Open Market Operation

2. Using of Indian rupee for international trade

Dollar-strapped Sri Lanka and sanctions-hit Russia will be the first countries to use the Indian rupee trade settlement mechanism, a game-changing initiative that allows using Indian rupees instead of dollars and other major currencies for International transactions.
India’s Central Bank has approved banks to open 05 special rupee trading accounts, called Vostro accounts, for trade with Sri Lanka through the Indian rupee trade settlement mechanism. Similarly, the Reserve Bank of India has given approval to banks to open 9 ‘Vostro’ for trade in rupees with Russia. Another account for trade with Mauritius has also been authorized by the RBI, which set up the mechanism in July.

Sri Lanka
The move is significant for Sri Lanka who is starved of foreign exchange reserves. As a lion’s share of global trade is conducted in Union States dollars, two countries would normally export and import from each other in US dollars but also in Euro, the British pound or Yen to a lesser degree. The option to trade with India in Indian rupees offers Sri Lanka respite from dipping into its scarce foreign exchange reserves after months of economic turmoil.
Sri Lanka currently permits citizens to have $10,000 worth of foreign currency on them, down from an earlier limit of $15,000 to save on foreign exchange. Through these arrangements, India has permitted Sri Lankan citizens to now hold these $10,000 in Indian rupees (nearly ₹8.14 lakhs) in physical form.
Factors like COVID-19 pandemic, governmental misstep and political unrest has led Sri Lanka into economic crisis, resulted to lack of foreign currency reserves, shortage of essentials and protests against the government. This led Sri Lanka to borrow reserves for larger countries like China, India and Japan. In September, it stuck a staff-level agreement with the International Monetary Fund. However, there is no reports to show that the Indian rupee may supplement the Sri Lankan rupee as legal tender in Sri Lanka (legal tender is a currency that can be used to settle domestic transactions or debt).

Russia
Russia is currently subject to various international sanctions due to its military operations in Ukraine that restricts its ability to trade internationally in prominent global reserve currencies mainly usage of US Dollars. It has granted India - a major energy importer - discounts on oil and the option to trade with it in Indian rupees.

What are these special vostro accounts?
The RBI introduced the concept of these special rupee vostro accounts in July. These accounts facilitate 'correspondent banking': where an entity (in this case a bank in India) holds and operates an account on behalf of someone else (a foreign entity, normally a foreign bank).
Vostro accounts will be operated by select banks in India but it can also be operated by a foreign bank through its Indian branch. For a foreign entity to open such an account, it must first approach an authorized bank (authorized dealer bank or AD bank) in India. The AD bank must then present its case before the RBI and get its approval to open and operate such an account.
All export and import invoicing between the two countries may be carried out in Indian rupees. When an Indian exporter want to export to the country in question, the exporters can receive Indian rupees from the balance in these vostro accounts. Indian importers who wish to import from such countries may directly deposit Indian rupees into these accounts.
The RBI also suggests that there are no restrictions on remitting the balances of these accounts, and that converting them into foreign currencies would be possible at market determined rates. Surplus balances in these accounts can be invested in government securities or payments for projects and investments.

Is this the first time India is carrying out foreign trade in Indian rupees?
No, as a portion of India's trade with Bhutan and Nepal are already carried out in Indian rupees. India have undertaken trade with Iran to settle energy transaction in the Indian rupees in 2013 and 2018. This arrangement was more limited, chiefly to bypass US sanctions on Iran by trading in Euro and Indian rupees.
The sanctions on Iran were lifted in 2015 after China, Russia, UK, France, The European Union and the US made an agreement with Iran on oversight over the latter’s contentious nuclear program. But they were re-imposed in 2018 by US under the President Donald Trump when they unilaterally reneged from the agreement.
The vostro account program differs from these earlier attempts to trade in the Indian rupees by giving it greater international prominence by facilitating broader trade with any country.

Conclusion
The agreement of usage of Indian rupee for foreign trade with Russia and Sri Lanka is little bit easy, but convincing other countries is so easy because we are importing more commodities than exporting with 35 countries mainly with Saudi Arabia and UAE. In every financial year there is Approx. $12 billion dollar surplus with UAE and Approx. $20 billion dollar with Saudi Arabia. The main problem is that, if the surplus created in the trade is in dollars then the surplus amount can be used for payments with other countries, but the surplus created will be in Indian rupees then that amount cannot be used for trade with other countries.
To solve this problem RBI and Indian government has to make several policies to reduce the deficit in foreign trade. Basically we must increase exports, increase utility of rupee and incentivize foreign investors.

3. ಭರತನಾಟ್ಯ

ಭರತನಾಟ್ಯವು ಕೇವಲ ಒಂದು ಮನೋರಂಜನೆಯ ಭಾಗವಲ್ಲ ಅದು ಒಬ್ಬ ನೃತ್ಯಗಾರ ಅಥವಾ ನೃತ್ಯಗಾತಿಗೆ ಕೇವಲ ನೃತ್ಯವಾಗಿರುವುದಿಲ್ಲ, ಅದು ಭಾವನೆಗಳನ್ನು ವ್ಯಕ್ತ ಪಡಿಸುವ ಹಾಗೂ ದುಃಖಗಳನ್ನು ಮರೆಸುವ ಸಾಮರ್ಥ್ಯ ಮತ್ತು ಹಲವು ಬಾರಿ ಜೀವನ ಪಾಠ ಕಲಿಸುವ ಗುರು ಆಗಿರುತ್ತದೆ.
ಭಾರತನಾಟ್ಯವು ದಕ್ಷಿಣ ಭಾರತದ ಜನಪ್ರಿಯ ಶಾಸ್ತ್ರೀಯ ನೃತ್ಯ ಪ್ರಕಾರವಾಗಿದೆ. ಭರತನಾಟ್ಯ ಪ್ರದರ್ಶನವು ಕಣ್ಣಿನ ಚಲನೆಗಳು, ಅಭಿವ್ಯಕ್ತಿಗಳು, ಕೈ ಸನ್ನೆಗಳು, ಹೆಜ್ಜೆಗಳು, ಸಂಗೀತ ಮತ್ತು ನೃತ್ಯಗಳ ಸಮಾಗಮದಿಂದ ನೋಡುಗರಿಗೆ ವಿಭಿನ್ನ ಅನುಭವ ನೀಡುತ್ತದೆ.

ಭರತನಾಟ್ಯದ ಉಗಮ:
ಪುರಾಣವು ಹೇಳುವಂತೆ ಬ್ರಹ್ಮನಿಂದ ನಿರ್ಮಿತವಾದ ನಾಟ್ಯವೇದಕ್ಕೆ, ಶಸ್ತ್ರರೂಪವನ್ನು ಕೊಟ್ಟು ಭರತನು ನಾಟ್ಯಶಾಸ್ತ್ರವನ್ನು ತನ್ನ ನೂರು ಮಕ್ಕಳಿಗೆ ಭೋದಿಸಿದನು. "ಶಿವನಿಂದ ತಾಂಡವ, ಪಾರ್ವತಿಯ ಮೂಲಕ ಉಷೆಯಿಂದ ಲಾಸ್ಯದ ವಿವರಣೆ ಪಡೆದು ಹೀಗೆ ಅನೇಕ ಗಂಧರ್ವ ಹಾಗೂ ಅಪ್ಸರೆಯರಿಗೆ ಈ ಭರತನಾಟ್ಯ ನೃತ್ಯದ ಶಿಕ್ಷಣವನ್ನು ನೀಡಿದನು. ಕ್ರಮೇಣ ಈ ಕಲೆಯು ಭೂಲೋಕದಲ್ಲಿ ಪಸರಿಸಿ, ರಾಜಮಹಾರಾಜರಿಂದ ಮನ್ನಣೆ ಪಡೆದು ಭಾರತದಲ್ಲಿ ಪ್ರಬಲ ಸ್ತನವನ್ನು ಪಡೆದುಕೊಂಡಿದೆ. ದೇಶದ ಬೇರೆ ಬೇರೆ ಪ್ರದೇಶಗಳಲ್ಲಿ "ಸಾಧಿರ್", "ಚಿನ್ನಮೆಲನ್", "ದಾಸಿಯಾಟ್ಟಮ್" ಎಂಬ ಹೆಸರುಗಳಿಂದ ರೂಪ ತಾಳಿ,ದಕ್ಷಿಣ ಭಾರತದಲ್ಲಿ "ಭರತನಾಟ್ಯ" ಎಂಬ ಹೆಸರು ಈ ನೃತ್ಯಕ್ಕೆ ಬಂದಿದೆ.
ಅಂದಿನಿಂದ ಈ ನೃತ್ಯವನ್ನು ದೇವಸ್ಥಾನಗಳಲ್ಲಿ ನರ್ತಿಸುವ ಪದ್ಧತಿ ಬೆಳೆದು ಬಂದಿತು, ಹೀಗೆ ನರ್ತಿಸುವವರನ್ನು "ಜೀವ್" ಎಂದು ಕರೆಯಲಾಗುತಿತ್ತು. ಇವರು ದಿನನಿತ್ಯ ದೇವಸ್ಥಾನದ ಕೆಲಸಗಳಲ್ಲಿ ಪಾಲ್ಗೊಳ್ಳುತ್ತಿದ್ದರು, ಅಲ್ಲದೆ ಉತ್ಸವಗಳ ಕಾಲದಲ್ಲಿ ನರ್ತಿಸುತ್ತಿದರು. ಭರತನಾಟ್ಯದ ನೃತ್ಯ ಮಾಡುವವರಿಗೆ ರಾಜಾಶ್ರಯ ದೊರೆಯುತ್ತಿತ್ತು, ಇಂದಿಗೂ ಈ ದೈವಿಕವಾದ ನೃತ್ಯದ ಬೆಳವಣಿಗೆ ಸಂವೃದ್ದವಾಗಿ ಬೆಳೆದಿದೆ. ಈ ನೃತ್ಯ ಪಾಶ್ಚಾತ್ಯ ದೇಶಗಳಿಗೂ ಹರಡಿ ಸಂದ ಗೌರವ ಹಾಗೂ ಖ್ಯಾತಿಯನ್ನು ಪಡೆದಿದೆ.

ಭರತನಾಟ್ಯದಲ್ಲಿ "ನಾಟ್ಯ", "ನೃತ್ಯ", "ನೃತ್ತ" ಎಂಬ ಮೂರು ಭಾಗಗಳಿವೆ.
ನಾಟ್ಯ: ನಾಟ್ಯ ಎಂದರೆ ನಾಟಕೀಯ ಪ್ರಾತಿನಿಧ್ಯ ಅಥವಾ ಭಾಷಣ, ಸಂಗೀತ ಮತ್ತು ನೃತ್ಯದೊಂದಿಗೆ ನಾಟಕ ಎಂದರ್ಥ.
ನೃತ್ಯ: ಭಾವನೆ ಮತ್ತು ಮಾನಸಿಕ ಸ್ಥಿತಿಗಳಿಗೆ ಸಂಬಂಧಿಸಿದ ಭಾಗವನ್ನು ನೃತ್ಯ ಎಂದು ಕರೆಯುತ್ತಾರೆ.
ನೃತ್ತ: ಪದಗಳು ಮತ್ತು ನಾಟಕೀಯ ಮನಸ್ಥಿತಿಗಳು ಅಥವಾ ಭಾವನೆಗಳಿಲ್ಲದೆ ಕೈಕಾಲುಗಳ ಲಯಬದ್ಧ ಚಲನೆಯನ್ನು ಹೊಂದಿರುವ ಒಂದು ರೀತಿಯ ನೃತ್ಯ ಪ್ರಸ್ತುತಿ.

ಭರತನಾಟ್ಯವನ್ನು ಕಲಿಯುವ ಕ್ರಮ :-
ಮೊದಿಲಿಗೆ ನಮಸ್ಕಾರ ಮಾಡುವ ವಿಧಾನದ ಪಾಠ, ಅದರ ಪ್ರಾಮುಖ್ಯತೆಯ ತಿಳುವಳಿಕೆ, ಆನಂತರ ವಿವಿಧ ಅಡುವುಗಳ ಪಾಠ, ಜೊತೆಗೆ ಅಂಗಾಂಗಗಳನ್ನು ಬಾಗಿಸಲು ಅನುಕೂಲವಾಗುವ ವ್ಯಾಯಾಮ ಕ್ರಿಯೆಗಳ ಪಾಠ. ಇಲ್ಲಿ "ಅಡುವು" ಎಂದರೆ ಮೂಲಭೂತದ ಹೆಜ್ಜೆ ಎಂದರ್ಥ. ಅಡವಿನ ಬಗ್ಗೆ ಹೇಳುವಾಗ ಕಾಲುಗಳ ಚಾಲನೆಯಿಂದ ಪ್ರಾರಂಭಿಸಿ ಆಯಾ ಅಡವಿಗೆ ಹಸ್ತಗಳ ಜೋಡಣೆ, ಚಲನೆ ಹಾಗೂ ಅವುಗಳಿಗೆ ಅನುಗುಣವಾದ ಶಿರ, ಗ್ರೀವ, ದೃಷ್ಟಿಗಳ ಚಲನೆ, ದೇಹದ ಬಾಗುವಿಕೆ ಇತ್ಯಾದಿಗಳು ತಿಳಿಯುತ್ತದೆ.

ಭರತನಾಟ್ಯದಲ್ಲಿ ಬಳಸಲಾಗುವ ವಾದ್ಯಗಳು:
ನಾಟುವಾಂಗದ ಕಬ್ಬಿಣ ಹಾಗೂ ಕಂಚಿನ ತಾಳ, ಮೃದಂಗ, ವೀಣೆ, ತಂಬೂರಿ ಇವುಗಳನ್ನು ಹಿಂದಿನ ಕಾಲದಲ್ಲಿ ಉಪಯೋಗಿಸುತ್ತಿದ್ದರು. ಈಗ ಎಲೆಕ್ಟ್ರಾನಿಕ್ ತಂಬೂರಿ, ಕ್ಷಾರಿನೆಟ್, ಪಿಟೀಲು, ಕೊಳಲು, ಮೋರ್ಚಿಂಗ್ ಹಾಗೂ ವಿಶೇಷ ಪರಿಣಾಮಗಳಿಗಾಗಿ ಖಾಂಜಿರ, ಘಟಿಕ, ಚಂಡೆ, ಕೀ-ಬೋರ್ಡ್, ಇನ್ನು ಹತ್ತು ಹಲವು ವಾದ್ಯಗಳನ್ನು ಬೆಳೆಸುತ್ತಾರೆ. ನೃತ್ಯವನ್ನು ಕಲಿಯುವುದಕ್ಕೆ ಯಾವುದೇ ತರಹದ ವಯಸ್ಸಿನ ಮಿತಿ ಇರುವುದಿಲ್ಲ, ಯಾವ ವಯಸ್ಸಿನವರಾದರೂ ನೃತ್ಯವನ್ನು ಕಲಿಯಬಹುದು.
ಈ ನೃತ್ಯವನ್ನು ಕಲಿವರು ಖಿನ್ನತೆಯಿಂದ ಹೊರಬರಲು ಹಾಗೂ ಕೋಪವನ್ನು ಹಿಡಿತದಲ್ಲಿಟ್ಟುಕೊಳ್ಳುವುದರಲ್ಲಿ ಹಾಗೂ ಮಾನಸಿಕ ಸಂತಸ, ಸ್ಥಿರತೆಯನ್ನು ಪಡೆದುಕೊಳ್ಳುವುದರಲ್ಲಿ ಮೇಲುಗೈ ಸಾಧಿಸಿದ್ದಾರೆ. ಭರತನಾಟ್ಯವನ್ನು ಕಲಿಯಲು ನೃತ್ಯದ ಮೇಲೆ ಆಸಕ್ತಿ ಮತ್ತು ಏಕಾಗ್ರತೆ ಬಹಳ ಮುಖ್ಯ. ನೃತ್ಯಕ್ಕೆ ಒಬ್ಬ ವ್ಯಕ್ತಿಯನ್ನು ಸಂಪೂರ್ಣವಾಗಿ ಬದಲಾಯಿಸಿ ಒಳ್ಳೆಯ ದಾರಿಯಲ್ಲಿ ನಡೆಸುವ ಶಕ್ತಿ ಇದೆ. ಭರತನಾಟ್ಯ ಅಭ್ಯಾಸ ಮಾಡುವುದರಿಂದ ಜೀವನದ ಎಲ್ಲಾ ಭಾಗಗಳ ಮೇಲೆ ಆಸಕ್ತಿ ಹೆಚ್ಚಾಗುತ್ತದೆ.

4. Impact of COVID 19 on Education system

Corona virus is a contagious disease caused by a virus ,the severe acute respiratory syndrome, coronavirus 2(SARS-COV-2). The first known case what identified in Wuhan, China in December 2019. The disease quickly spread worldwide, resulting in the COVID 19 pandemic.

The symptoms of covid 19 are variable but often include fever, cough, headache, fatigue, breathing difficulties, loss of smell, loss of taste etc. COVID 19 transmits when people breate air ,contaminated by droplets and small airborne particles containing the virus. Transmission can also occur if contaminated fluids are splashed or sprayed in the eyes nose or mouth.

The Impact of covid19 is observed in every sector and every field of life. Almost all government’s decided to temporarily close all educational institutions and implemented localized closure to reduce the spread of covid 19.

The educational sector in india, which was hitherto slow to change, has been witnessing a massive transformation recently with changing job landscape, technological disruptions, demand for quality education and the implementation of National Educational Policy (NEP)2020. The pandemic caused further shocks to the system ,with schools forced to shut down during the lockdown period, and the transition of students and teachers to online teaching and learning.

In india ,around 250 million students were affected due to school closures , at the onset of lockdown induced by COVID 19. The pandemic posed several challenges in public and private school which included an expected rise in dropouts, learning losses and increase in digital divide.

The changes of class room to digital learning at the outbreak of pandemic disturbed the learning of children in low-income area around the world. Students from the weaker section of the society who could not afford smartphones, wifi, computer or laptop were at immediate disadvantage. As the method changes to digital learning the main question here is , a students are actually learning anything?? One of the biggest disadvantage of online learning is that many students struggle to focus for long duration on screen. Not to mention, there is a plethora of distracting content available online with attracts and distracts the students. Parents found that many students are skipping classes and playing games. The students had become socially isolated during online learning.

Students were promoted to next class without any examination and the students were given marks based on the marks obtained by them in the previous classes. This hampered the quality of education and at the same time it will have bad effect on the future of the children. Many pass out students lost important job opportunities and most of the recruitment got hampered because of the pandemic. Placement for students were affected.

Unemployment has been rising since the COVID 19 pandemic and this pushed the parents to prioritise survival over children’s education. As per the report submitted by the educational ministry, around 15 crore children were out of education system. The Unified District Information System for Education (UDISF) reported similar numbers. The figure for dropouts at the secondary level was high as 17% and then, there were children who have never been enrolled. Also, girl students from low-income families and rural areas were at a higher risk of sexual abuse, and forced labor, and early marriag. These challenges amplified with the impact of temporary school closure due to Covid 19.

Finally, the COVID-19 pandemic had impacted the face-to-face education system. The schools and universities have designed strategies and methods to recover lost portions, ensure children return to school when schools reopen, and scale online learning infrastructures.

NEP 2020, and subsequent government initiatives such as national digital education architecture (ndear) and national initiative for proficiency in reading with understanding and numeracy (nipun bharat) are expected to provide a blueprint for the transformation.
